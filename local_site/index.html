Fast Fashion & Climate Justice

Fashion’s hidden cost—on our planet and our people

Fast fashion accelerates carbon emissions, drains water, and creates waste—while workers and communities on the frontlines bear the brunt. This site brings the impacts into focus and shows how you can help.

See the impact Take action

At a glance

🧵
Overproduction: Millions of tons of clothing made yearly—much ends up landfilled or burned.
💧
Water stress: Cotton and dyeing strain rivers and aquifers in already-vulnerable regions.
🌍
Unequal burdens: Low-income and marginalized communities face pollution, heat, and unsafe work.

How fast fashion fuels the climate crisis

🔥
Emissions along the supply chain

From fiber production to shipping and returns, each step burns energy—often from fossil fuels.

🧪
Toxic dyes & microfibers

Wastewater and shedding microfibers pollute waterways, harming ecosystems and human health.

🗑️
Take-make-waste model

Ultra-fast trends push overconsumption, creating mountains of textile waste and open-air dumps.

Who is most affected?

👩🏽‍🏭
Garment workers

Precarious jobs, heat stress, and exposure to chemicals.

🏘️
Fence-line communities

Neighborhoods near mills, tanneries, and dumps inhale pollution daily.

🌊
Climate-vulnerable regions

Droughts and floods hit hardest where resources are already scarce.

What you can do

The journey of a garment

A single t-shirt can touch farms, mills, dye houses, cut-and-sew factories, warehouses, and long-haul shipping—multiplying its footprint. Deliberate choices at each step can cut emissions, water use, and harm.

🌾
Fiber: Prefer organic/regenerative or recycled fibers when possible.
⚡
Manufacturing: Electrify processes and source clean power.
🔁
Use phase: Wear longer, wash cooler, repair often.
